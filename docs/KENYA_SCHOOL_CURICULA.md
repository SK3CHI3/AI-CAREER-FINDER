# PART ONE
## COMPETENCY-BASED CURRICULUM (CBC)

### 1. Overview & Background
The Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) is Kenya's national curriculum framework, introduced in 2017 by the Ministry of Education to replace the former 8-4-4 system. It is developed and managed by the Kenya Institute of Curriculum Development (KICD) and implemented across all public schools and the majority of private schools in Kenya.

**Key Philosophy:** CBC shifts focus from rote memorisation and high-stakes exams to the development of practical competencies, critical thinking, creativity, communication, collaboration, and digital literacy — skills needed in the 21st century.

- **Governing Bodies:** Ministry of Education | KICD | Kenya National Examinations Council (KNEC)
- **Introduced:** 2017 (replacing 8-4-4 system)
- **Language of Instruction:** English and Kiswahili

**Core CBC Values & Competencies:**
| Competency | Description |
|---|---|
| Communication & Collaboration | Working effectively with others and expressing ideas clearly |
| Critical Thinking & Problem Solving | Analysing situations and developing solutions |
| Creativity & Imagination | Generating novel ideas and innovative approaches |
| Citizenship | Responsible participation in society and community |
| Digital Literacy | Effective and ethical use of technology |
| Learning to Learn | Self-directed learning and lifelong education habits |
| Self-Efficacy | Confidence and resilience in one's own abilities |

### 2. CBC Structure & Levels
CBC is structured into four broad levels, replacing the old 8-4-4 model of 8 years primary + 4 years secondary + 4 years university.

| Level | Classes | Age Group | Duration | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Early Years | Pre-Primary 1 (PP1), PP2, Grades 1–3 | Ages 4–9 | 5 years | Foundational literacy, numeracy & social skills |
| Middle School | Grades 4–6 | Ages 10–12 | 3 years | Broadening knowledge, introduction to subjects |
| Junior Secondary | Grades 7–9 | Ages 13–15 | 3 years | Core subjects + electives. Ends with KLEE exam |
| Senior Secondary | Grades 10–12 | Ages 16–18 | 3 years | Pathway specialisation: STEM / Arts / Social Sciences |
| University | Undergraduate | Ages 18+ | 3–5 years | Degree programmes at Kenyan universities |

### 4. CBC Assessments & National Examinations
CBC uses a multi-layered assessment approach combining continuous school-based assessments with periodic national examinations administered by KNEC. Unlike the old 8-4-4 system, no single exam determines a learner's future.

**4a. EGRA — Early Grade Reading Assessment**
- Full Name: Early Grade Reading Assessment
- Level: Grades 2 and 3 (Ages 7–9)
- Administered by: Ministry of Education in partnership with KICD, USAID & RTI International
- When: Periodically during Term 1 or Term 2 — sample-based, not every school every year
- Purpose: Diagnostic only — does NOT affect promotion or grading

| Skill Assessed | Description |
|---|---|
| Letter Recognition | Can the child identify letters of the alphabet? |
| Phonemic Awareness | Can they identify sounds within words? |
| Decoding | Can they sound out unfamiliar words correctly? |
| Reading Fluency | How many words can they read correctly per minute? |
| Reading Comprehension | Do they understand what they have read? |

*EGRA is assessed in both English and Kiswahili. Results inform national literacy policy, teacher training interventions, and government/NGO resource allocation. It is purely diagnostic — results are never used to hold back or fail a learner.*

**4b. KLEE — Kenya Lower Secondary Examination**
- Full Name: Kenya Lower Secondary Examination
- Level: End of Grade 9 (Junior Secondary — Ages ~15)
- Administered by: Kenya National Examinations Council (KNEC)
- First administered: 2026
- Purpose: Certifies completion of Junior Secondary; results used for Senior Secondary placement & pathway selection

| Subject | Assessment Mode |
|---|---|
| English | Written exam + oral component |
| Kiswahili / Kenya Sign Language | Written exam + oral component |
| Mathematics | Written exam — two papers |
| Integrated Science | Written exam + practical |
| Health Education | Written exam |
| Pre-Technical & Pre-Career Education | Written + practical/portfolio |
| Social Studies | Written exam |
| Religious Education (CRE/IRE/HRE) | Written exam — student picks one |
| Business Studies | Written exam |
| Agriculture & Nutrition | Written exam + practical |
| Creative Arts (elective) | Portfolio + performance assessment |
| Physical Education (elective) | Performance-based assessment |

*Outcome: KLEE results determine which Senior Secondary pathway (STEM, Arts & Sports Science, or Social Sciences) a learner is placed into for Grades 10–12. It replaces the role the old KCPE played at the end of primary school.*

**4c. Grade 12 National Examination (KUSE)**
- Proposed Name: Kenya Upper Secondary Education Certificate (KUSE) / Kenya Secondary Education Certificate (KSEC)
- Level: End of Grade 12 (Senior Secondary — Ages ~18)
- Administered by: KNEC
- First cohort: 2028
- Note: The official branding was still being finalised by KNEC as of early 2026.

| Component | Weight | Description |
|---|---|---|
| National Written Exam | 40–50% | Sit-down papers set and marked by KNEC |
| School-Based Assessment (SBA) | 30–40% | Projects, practicals & portfolios done in school over Grades 10–12 |
| Competency Assessment | 10–20% | Skills demonstrations, fieldwork, presentations |

### 5. CBC Grading Scale
CBC uses a competency-level scale rather than the old A–E letter grades. This reflects a learner's demonstrated proficiency rather than a single exam score.

| CBC Level | Old 8-4-4 Equivalent | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Exceeds Expectation (EE) | Equivalent of old Grade A | Outstanding mastery of skills and content |
| Meets Expectation (ME) | Equivalent of old Grade B/C | Solid understanding and application |
| Approaches Expectation (AE) | Equivalent of old Grade D | Basic understanding with gaps |
| Below Expectation (BE) | Equivalent of old Grade E | Significant gaps requiring intervention |

# PART TWO
## BRITISH CURRICULUM (IGCSE / A-LEVELS)

### 7. Overview & Background
The British Curriculum in Kenya is administered by Cambridge Assessment International Education (CAIE), a department of the University of Cambridge. It is the most widely adopted international curriculum in Kenya, offered by mid-to-high-end private and international schools. It is globally recognised and provides a strong pathway to universities in the UK, Australia, Canada, and beyond.

- Awarding Body: Cambridge Assessment International Education (CAIE) — University of Cambridge
- Language of Instruction: English (with optional second languages)
- Global Recognition: Accepted by universities in 160+ countries
- Key Examinations: Cambridge Primary Checkpoint | Lower Secondary Checkpoint | IGCSE | AS & A-Level

### 8. British Curriculum Structure
| Stage | Age Group | Year Group | Key Features |
|---|---|---|---|
| Early Years (EYFS) | Ages 3–5 | Pre-Primary | Play-based; 7 development areas; no formal exams |
| Key Stage 1 (KS1) | Ages 5–7 | Years 1–2 | Core literacy & numeracy foundations |
| Key Stage 2 (KS2) | Ages 7–11| Years 3–6 | Primary subjects; ends with Cambridge Primary Checkpoint |
| Key Stage 3 (KS3) | Ages 11–14| Years 7–9 | Broader subjects; ends with Cambridge Lower Sec. Checkpoint |
| IGCSE | Ages 14–16| Years 10–11| 8–11 subjects; major national exam; equivalent to KLEE/KCSE |
| AS-Level | Ages 16–17| Year 12 | 3–4 specialised subjects; half of A-Level qualification |
| A-Level | Ages 17–18| Year 13 | Final qualification; gateway to university admission |

### 9. Subjects by Stage

**Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) — Ages 3–5**
No formal subject teaching. Learning is organised around seven areas of development:
- Personal, Social & Emotional Development
- Communication & Language
- Physical Development
- Literacy (early reading & writing)
- Mathematics (counting, shapes, patterns)
- Understanding the World
- Expressive Arts & Design

**Key Stage 1 & 2 (Primary) — Ages 5–11**
| Subject | Description |
|---|---|
| English Language | Reading, Writing, Speaking & Listening |
| Mathematics | Number, Algebra, Geometry, Statistics |
| Science | Biology, Chemistry & Physics integrated |
| History | Local, national & world history |
| Geography | Physical & human geography |
| Art & Design | Drawing, painting, sculpture, craft |
| Music | Singing, instruments, music theory |
| Physical Education | Sports, movement, health & fitness |
| ICT / Computing | Digital skills, coding basics |
| Religious Education | Comparative world religions |
| PSHE | Personal, Social, Health & Economic Education |
| Modern Foreign Lang. | French, Kiswahili, or Mandarin (school-dependent) |

**Key Stage 3 — Ages 11–14 (Years 7–9)**
| Subject | Description |
|---|---|
| English Language & Lit | Advanced reading, writing, analysis |
| Mathematics | Algebra, geometry, statistics, probability |
| Sciences | Biology, Chemistry, Physics (separate or combined) |
| History | World & British history, source analysis |
| Geography | Ecosystems, development, climate |
| Religious Studies | Ethics, world religions |
| Art & Design | Projects, techniques, art history |
| Music | Composition, performance, listening |
| Drama | Performance, script writing, stagecraft |
| Physical Education | Team & individual sports |
| ICT / Computer Science | Programming, digital systems |
| Design & Technology | Product design, engineering principles |
| Modern Foreign Lang. | French, Spanish, Kiswahili |
| PSHE | Life skills, health, citizenship |

**IGCSE — Ages 14–16 (Years 10–11)**
Students typically choose 8–11 subjects from six subject groups. Most Kenyan schools require English, Mathematics, at least one Science, one Humanity, and one Language.

| Subject Group | Subjects Available |
|---|---|
| Group 1 — Languages | English First Language, English Second Language, French, Kiswahili, Arabic, Spanish |
| Group 2 — Humanities | History, Geography, Religious Studies, Sociology, Travel & Tourism |
| Group 3 — Sciences | Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Combined Science, Environmental Science |
| Group 4 — Mathematics | Mathematics (Core & Extended), Additional Mathematics |
| Group 5 — Creative & Voc. | Art & Design, Music, Drama, ICT, Computer Science, Business Studies, Economics, Accounting |
| Group 6 — Physical Ed. | Physical Education (coursework & written paper) |

**A-Levels — Ages 16–18 (Years 12–13)**
Students specialise in 3–4 subjects chosen to align with their intended university course. This is the British curriculum's final qualification before university.

| Category | Available Subjects |
|---|---|
| Sciences & Maths | Mathematics, Further Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Computer Science |
| Humanities | History, Geography, Economics, Business, Psychology, Sociology, Law |
| Languages | English Language, English Literature, French, Kiswahili |
| Arts | Art & Design, Music, Drama & Theatre Studies |

### 10. National Examinations

**10a. Cambridge Primary Checkpoint**
- Level: End of Year 6 (Key Stage 2) — Ages ~11
- Subjects Tested: English, Mathematics, Science
- When: April/May each year
- Format: Written papers set and marked by Cambridge
- Purpose: Diagnostic — measures readiness for KS3; provides international benchmarking
- Mandatory? Optional but widely used by British curriculum schools in Kenya

**10b. Cambridge Lower Secondary Checkpoint**
- Level: End of Year 9 (Key Stage 3) — Ages ~14
- Subjects Tested: English, Mathematics, Science
- When: April/May each year
- Format: Written papers
- Purpose: Assesses readiness for IGCSE; helps schools guide subject selection
- Grading: Scale of 0–6 with detailed feedback reports

**10c. IGCSE Examinations**
- Level: End of Year 11 — Ages ~16
- Administered by: Cambridge Assessment International Education (CAIE)
- Main Session (Kenya): May/June each year
- Alternative Session: October/November
- Results Released: August (May/June sitting)
- Mandatory for: Progression to A-Levels and many university applications

| Chemistry | Paper 1: MCQ \| Paper 2: Theory \| Paper 3: Practical |

*IGCSE Grading Scale:*
| Grade | Description | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| A* | Outstanding | Highest possible grade — exceptional performance |
| A | Excellent | Very strong understanding and application |
| B | Very Good | Above average performance |

*A minimum of 5 subjects graded C or above (including English and Mathematics) is the standard requirement for A-Level entry at most Kenyan British curriculum schools.*

**10d. A-Level / AS-Level Examinations**
- Level: End of Year 12 (AS) and Year 13 (A-Level) — Ages 17–18

*A-Level Exam Format:* Each A-Level subject typically has 2–3 written papers plus coursework or practical assessments. Papers are set and marked by Cambridge examiners internationally.

# PART THREE
## INTERNATIONAL BACCALAUREATE (IB)

### 13. Overview & Background
The International Baccalaureate (IB) is a globally prestigious, non-profit educational foundation headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland. Founded in 1968, it offers four internationally recognised programmes for students aged 3–19. In Kenya, the IB is offered by a select group of premium international and private schools, primarily in Nairobi. It is widely considered the most rigorous and globally recognised pre-university curriculum in the world, accepted by leading universities across all continents.

- Awarding Body: International Baccalaureate Organization (IBO) — Geneva, Switzerland
- Founded: 1968
- Language of Instruction: English, French, or Spanish (English in Kenya)
- Global Recognition: Accepted by universities in 90+ countries including top Ivy League, Oxbridge, and Russell Group
- Programmes: PYP | MYP | DP (Diploma) | CP (Career-related)
- Cost in Kenya: Very High — premium international school fees (KES 1.5M–4M+ per year)

### 14. IB Programmes & Structure
| Programme | Age Group | School Level | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary Years Programme (PYP) | Ages 3–12 | Early childhood / primary | Inquiry-based, transdisciplinary framework |
| Middle Years Programme (MYP) | Ages 11–16| Lower to upper secondary | Connects PYP to Diploma; broad subject range |
| Diploma Programme (DP) | Ages 16–18| Senior secondary (2 years) | Flagship qualification — globally recognised |
| Career-related Programme (CP)| Ages 16–18| Senior secondary (2 years) | Combines IB subjects with vocational training |

**14a. Primary Years Programme (PYP) — Ages 3–12**
The PYP is a transdisciplinary framework that focuses on the development of the whole child.
*PYP Subject Areas:* Language, Mathematics, Social Studies, Science, Arts, Physical & Health Education.
*Assessment:* Portfolio-based. Ends with the PYP Exhibition (Grade 6).

**14b. Middle Years Programme (MYP) — Ages 11–16**
Bridges PYP and DP. Encourages practical connections.
*MYP Groups:* Language & Literature, Language Acquisition, Individuals & Societies, Sciences, Mathematics, Arts, Physical & Health Education, Design.
*Assessment:* Primarily school-based. Optional MYP eAssessments in Year 5.

**14c. IB Diploma Programme (IBDP) — Ages 16–18**
Rigorous two-year programme. Students study six subjects simultaneously across different discipline groups + three mandatory core components.

**14d. IB Career-related Programme (CP) — Ages 16–18**
Combines IB Diploma subjects with practical career-related learning (e.g. hospitality, IT, business).
*CP Core:* Personal & Professional Skills (PPS), Service Learning, Reflective Project, Language Development.

### 15. IBDP Subjects & Groups
IBDP students choose one subject from each of six subject groups. Three subjects at Higher Level (HL) and three at Standard Level (SL).

| Subject Group | Subjects Available | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Group 1 — Studies in Language & Literature | Language A: Literature, Language A: Lang & Lit, Lit & Performance | Mother tongue / first language |
| Group 2 — Language Acquisition | Language B, Language Ab Initio | Second / additional language |
| Group 3 — Individuals & Societies | History, Geography, Economics, Psychology, Business, Philosophy, Global Politics | Humanities and social sciences |
| Group 4 — Sciences | Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Computer Science, Env Systems | Natural sciences |
| Group 5 — Mathematics | Math Analysis & Approaches (AA), Math Applications & Interpretation (AI) | Theoretical vs Practical |
| Group 6 — The Arts | Visual Arts, Music, Theatre, Film, Dance | Creative arts (can be swapped) |

*Common IBDP Combinations:*
| Career Goal | Typical Subject Combination |
|---|---|
| Medicine / Health | Biology (HL), Chemistry (HL), Mathematics AA (HL), English A (SL), French B (SL), Psychology (SL) |
| Engineering | Physics (HL), Mathematics AA (HL), Chemistry (HL), English A (SL), French B (SL), Comp Sci (SL) |
| Business / Econ | Economics (HL), Business Mgt (HL), Mathematics AI (HL), English A (SL), French B (SL), History (SL) |
| Law / Politics | History (HL), Global Politics (HL), English A (HL), Economics (SL), Mathematics AI (SL), French (SL) |

### 16. IBDP Core Components
| Core Component | Description | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Theory of Knowledge (TOK) | Philosophy course exploring the nature of knowledge. 1,600-word essay + exhibition. | Compulsory |
| Extended Essay (EE) | Independent 4,000-word research essay. | Compulsory |
| Creativity, Activity, Service (CAS) | 150 hours of arts/physical/service pursuits. Pass/Fail. | Compulsory |

### 17. IB Examinations & Assessments
- Administered by: IBO
- Main Session (Kenya): May each year
- Assessment Structure: External Assessment (EA) 60–80% (sit-down exams), Internal Assessment (IA) 20–40% (school-based work).

### 18. IB Grading Scale
The IBDP uses a 1–7 scale for each subject (max 42 points) + up to 3 bonus points from TOK & EE. Minimum 24 points to pass.

| Grade | Descriptor | Approx. % | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| 7 | Excellent | 90–100% | Outstanding performance |
| 6 | Very Good | 80–89% | Strong performance above standard |
| 5 | Good | 70–79% | Above average — meets university requirements |
| 4 | Satisfactory| 60–69% | Adequate performance — minimum for most courses |
| 3 | Mediocre | 50–59% | Limited achievement |
| 2 | Poor | 40–49% | Below standard |
| 1 | Very Poor | 0–39% | Minimal performance |

*Typical Scores for University:*
- Oxbridge / Ivy League: 40–45 points
- Russell Group UK / Top US: 36–39 points
- Good universities globally: 30–35 points
- Kenyan universities (direct entry): 24+ points

### 21. University Pathways
